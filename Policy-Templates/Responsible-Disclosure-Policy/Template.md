## Purpose

This policy defines how external security researchers can report vulnerabilities discovered in ____ products and services, and how internal Personnel can anonymously report information security policy violations.

## Scope

This policy covers ____ core products, services, and information security infrastructure. It applies to external security researchers, internal Personnel, and any third parties who discover or suspect a vulnerability.

## Background

____ is committed to ensuring the safety and security of our customers and employees. We recognize the importance of vulnerability disclosures and whistleblowers in maintaining security. This policy reflects our commitment to working with the security community in good faith.

## Legal Posture

____ will not pursue legal action against individuals who submit vulnerability reports in good faith through our designated reporting channel. This safe harbor applies to individuals who:

- Engage in testing or research without harming ____, its customers, or its systems.
- Test within the scope of our vulnerability disclosure program (see below).
- Do not access, modify, or delete data that does not belong to them.
- Adhere to applicable laws.
- Refrain from publicly disclosing vulnerability details before a mutually agreed-upon timeframe expires.

This safe harbor does not apply to individuals who engage in extortion, intentional destruction of data, or denial-of-service attacks.

## Policy

### Scope of Vulnerability Disclosure Program

The following systems and services are in scope:

- ____ (main application/platform)
- ____ (APIs and web services)
- ____ (public-facing infrastructure)

The following are explicitly out of scope:

- Social engineering or phishing attacks against ____ Personnel
- Physical security testing of ____ facilities
- Denial-of-service (DoS/DDoS) attacks
- Third-party applications or services not owned by ____

### How to Submit a Vulnerability Report

Submit vulnerability reports to: ____ (e.g., security@example.com, HackerOne, Bugcrowd)

Reports should include:

- A clear description of the vulnerability and its potential impact.
- Steps to reproduce the issue.
- Proof-of-concept code or screenshots (if applicable).
- Affected system, URL, or endpoint.
- Any plans for public disclosure.
- Contact information for follow-up (optional for anonymous submissions).

### What to Expect

- **Acknowledgment:** We will acknowledge receipt of your report within ____ business days.
- **Triage:** We will assess the report, validate the finding, and determine severity.
- **Timeline:** We will provide an expected remediation timeline and remain transparent about any challenges or delays.
- **Communication:** We will notify you when the vulnerability analysis completes each stage.
- **Credit:** We will publicly credit the researcher after the vulnerability has been validated and resolved, unless the researcher prefers to remain anonymous.
- **Dispute Resolution:** If communication issues arise, we may engage a neutral third party to facilitate resolution.

### Internal Whistleblowing

Personnel who suspect violations of information security policies, laws, or regulations may report them anonymously.

**How to Submit:**

- Use the organization's designated anonymous reporting channel: ____
- External anonymous email services may also be used.

**Protections:**

- Reports made in good faith will be investigated.
- The reporter's identity will be protected to the extent permitted by law.
  - Identity may need to be disclosed when required by law, a thorough investigation, or due process for accused individuals.
- Retaliation against whistleblowers is strictly prohibited and will result in disciplinary action.
- Protection against retaliation does not provide immunity for personal wrongdoing.

**What to Include in a Report:**

- Detailed description of the alleged violation (what, how, why).
- Facts about the reported event (who, where, when).
- You are not responsible for investigating or determining corrective measures.

## Prohibited Activities During Research

The following activities are prohibited during vulnerability research:

- Accessing, modifying, or deleting data that does not belong to the researcher.
- Exfiltrating data beyond what is necessary to demonstrate the vulnerability.
- Installing persistent malware or backdoors.
- Degrading service for other users.
- Using automated scanners at a rate that could impact service availability.

## Compliance and Enforcement

Violations of this policy by internal Personnel will be handled per the disciplinary process in the Information Security Policy. Good-faith external researchers who violate policy terms unintentionally will be notified and given the opportunity to correct their approach before any restrictions are imposed.
