## Purpose

This policy establishes requirements to ensure that ____ information assets are protected by physical controls that prevent tampering, damage, theft, or unauthorized physical access.

## Scope

This policy applies to all physical facilities, data centers, and workspaces used by ____, and to all Personnel, visitors, and external parties with physical access to organizational facilities.

## Policy

### General Physical Security

- Physical access to organizational facilities is restricted to authorized Personnel.
- All Personnel must wear visible identification badges in secure areas.
- On-site visitors and vendors must be escorted by an authorized employee at all times.
- Visitor access must be logged with name, organization, purpose of visit, time in/out, and escort name.
- Visitor logs must be retained for a minimum of ____ years.
- Building security measures (fire extinguishers, escape routes, emergency procedures) must comply with applicable laws and regulations.

### Access Requirements for Sensitive Areas

- Physical access to areas housing sensitive information or critical infrastructure must be controlled with badge readers, biometric systems, or smart locks that track access.
- Restricted areas must be locked when unattended.
- Access must be granted only to authorized Personnel and revoked upon termination or role change.
- Access rights must be reviewed at least ____ (quarterly/annually).
- Lost or stolen access badges or keys must be reported immediately. Access credentials must be revoked within ____ hours of report.
- Physical locks must be changed within ____ days of reporting lost or stolen keys.

### Workstation Security

- Workstations must only be used by authorized Personnel for job-related responsibilities.
- Personnel must lock workstations when unattended. Automatic screen locking must be configured with a maximum ____ minute idle timeout.
- All workstations purchased by the organization are organizational property and managed accordingly.
- Workstation hard drives must be encrypted per the Encryption Policy.

### Data Center Security

For organizations managing their own data centers:

- Physical access must be restricted to authorized operations personnel only.
- Access must be logged and reviewed periodically.
- Environmental controls (temperature, humidity, fire suppression) must be monitored and alarmed.
- Redundant power (UPS and generator) must be maintained and tested regularly.
- CCTV coverage must be maintained for all entry points and equipment areas.

For organizations using cloud service providers, physical data center security is the responsibility of the provider. The organization must obtain and review the provider's physical security attestations (SOC 2, ISO 27001) annually.

### Asset Security

- External threats (weather, malicious attacks, accidents) must be mitigated through appropriate risk controls.
- Environmental conditions (fire, smoke, temperature, humidity, power continuity) must be monitored and controlled in areas housing critical equipment.
- Continuous power supply (UPS) protection must be provided for critical information assets. Backup generators must be available for higher protection levels.
- Emergency power shut-off switches must be placed near emergency exits in equipment rooms and clearly labeled.

### Off-Site Equipment Security

- Equipment may only be taken off-site for valid business reasons and with authorization from the asset owner.
- Personnel are responsible for securing equipment and ensuring its protection at all times.
- Equipment taken off-site must be recorded upon removal and return, including asset tag, person responsible, date out, and date in.

### Cabling Protection

- Power and telecommunications cabling must be protected against interference, data capture, or physical damage.
- Cables must be clearly labeled to avoid handling errors.
- Cable runs through publicly accessible areas must be physically protected or enclosed.

### Alarm Systems

- Alarm systems must be installed, maintained, and periodically tested.
- Alarm systems must be monitored or configured to alert responsible personnel upon activation.
- System malfunctions must be addressed within ____ hours of detection.

## Compliance and Enforcement

Violation of this policy may result in disciplinary action as outlined in the Information Security Policy.
