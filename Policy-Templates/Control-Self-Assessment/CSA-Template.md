# Control Self-Assessment Process

Policy Title: Control Self-Assessment Process
Policy Number: ISP-____
Effective Date: ____
Version: 1.0
Classification: Internal
Approved By: ____

## Overview

____ is committed to maintaining a robust internal control environment to ensure the effectiveness and efficiency of operations, the reliability of reporting, and compliance with applicable laws, regulations, and industry frameworks. To this end, ____ performs regular control self-assessments to evaluate the adequacy and operating effectiveness of its internal controls.

## Purpose

This process establishes the requirements for conducting self-assessments of internal controls within ____. The assessments help identify control weaknesses, gaps, or potential improvements, ensure compliance with applicable framework commitments (e.g., SOC 2, ISO 27001, NIST 800-53, PCI DSS, HIPAA), and provide evidence for internal and external audits.

## Scope

This process applies to all internal controls and processes that are subject to compliance framework reporting at ____. This includes:

- Controls covering security, availability, processing integrity, confidentiality, and privacy (SOC 2 Trust Services Criteria).
- Controls mapped to applicable regulatory requirements.
- Controls documented in the organization's control framework or GRC platform.

## Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
|------|----------------|
| ____ (e.g., Security Officer / Compliance Officer) | Oversees the control self-assessment process; schedules assessments; reviews findings and corrective actions; reports to executive management |
| Department Heads / Control Owners | Conduct assessments within their respective areas; provide evidence of control effectiveness; address identified control deficiencies |
| Internal Audit / GRC Team | Provides support and oversight to ensure self-assessments are conducted in accordance with this process; assists in evaluating effectiveness of corrective actions |
| Executive Management | Reviews overall assessment outcomes; approves significant control changes or resource allocations |

## Assessment Cadence

Control self-assessments must be conducted at the following frequency:

- **Annual Comprehensive Assessment:** All in-scope controls assessed at least once per year.
- **Quarterly High-Risk Control Assessment:** Controls that address high-risk areas, have a history of deficiencies, or are critical to compliance must be assessed quarterly.
- **Event-Driven Assessment:** Controls are re-assessed upon:
  - Significant changes to the control environment (system changes, process changes, organizational restructuring).
  - Discovery of a control failure or incident related to the control.
  - Changes in applicable regulations or compliance framework requirements.
  - Findings from internal or external audits indicating control weakness.

## Procedure

### Step 1: Planning

The Security Officer (or Compliance Officer) is responsible for planning each assessment cycle:

- **Schedule:** Publish the assessment timeline at least ____ weeks before the assessment period begins. Include start date, evidence submission deadline, and review completion date.
- **Scope:** Define which controls are in scope for the assessment period. The scope may be all controls (annual) or a subset (quarterly high-risk, event-driven).
- **Assignments:** Assign each control to a responsible assessor (control owner or department head).
- **Communications:** Notify all control owners and stakeholders of the upcoming assessment, including expectations, deadlines, and required evidence types.
- **Methodology:** Confirm the assessment methodology and evidence requirements based on the applicable compliance framework(s).

### Step 2: Conducting Assessments

Each control owner (or designated representative) conducts a thorough review of assigned controls:

**Assessment Activities:**

1. **Review Control Description:** Verify that the control description in the GRC platform or control framework is accurate and reflects current operations. Update if necessary.
2. **Review Control Activity:** Confirm that the control is being performed as described:
   - What actions are taken?
   - Who performs them?
   - When and how frequently?
   - What tools or systems are used?
3. **Collect Evidence:** Gather evidence demonstrating that the control is operating effectively during the assessment period. Evidence may include:
   - System-generated reports or logs (e.g., access review reports, vulnerability scan results, change management logs).
   - Screenshots of configurations or dashboards.
   - Meeting minutes or approval records.
   - Policies, procedures, and training records.
   - Completed checklists or attestations.
   - Audit trails showing control execution.
4. **Evaluate Effectiveness:** For each control, determine:
   - **Operating Effectively:** The control is designed appropriately and is functioning as intended, with evidence to support this conclusion.
   - **Needs Improvement:** The control is partially effective; minor gaps or inconsistencies exist that require remediation.
   - **Not Effective:** The control is not achieving its objective; significant gaps or failures exist.
5. **Document Findings:** Record the assessment results in the GRC platform or designated system, including:
   - Assessment date and assessor name.
   - Control effectiveness rating.
   - Evidence references (uploaded or linked).
   - Observations, issues identified, and recommended corrective actions.
   - Any changes to the control description or implementation.

### Step 3: Reporting Findings

All findings from the self-assessments must be documented:

- **Effectiveness Summary:** A summary of control effectiveness ratings across all in-scope controls (count and percentage by rating: Effective, Needs Improvement, Not Effective).
- **Identified Issues:** Each issue must be documented with:
  - Issue description (what gap or deficiency was identified).
  - Severity (Critical, High, Medium, Low) based on the potential impact on compliance or security posture.
  - Affected control(s) and control owner.
  - Recommended corrective action.
  - Target remediation date.
- **Positive Observations:** Controls that exceed requirements or demonstrate best practices should be noted.

### Step 4: Review and Corrective Actions

The Security Officer reviews all completed assessments and findings:

- **Triage:** Validate the completeness and quality of assessments. Follow up on incomplete assessments or insufficient evidence.
- **Prioritization:** Prioritize corrective actions based on issue severity and regulatory/compliance impact.
- **Action Plans:** Collaborate with control owners to develop specific action plans for addressing identified deficiencies:
  - Description of corrective action.
  - Responsible party.
  - Target completion date.
  - Resources required.
  - Verification method (how will you confirm the action resolved the issue).
- **Approval:** Action plans for Critical and High severity issues must be approved by executive management.
- **Implementation:** Control owners implement corrective actions within the stipulated timeframe.

### Step 5: Follow-Up and Verification

The GRC team (or Internal Audit) conducts follow-up reviews:

- **Timing:** Follow-up occurs after the target completion date for each corrective action, or at the next quarterly review cycle, whichever is earlier.
- **Verification:** Verify that corrective actions have been implemented and are operating effectively:
  - Review updated control documentation.
  - Collect evidence of the implemented change.
  - Test the control if applicable.
- **Status Updates:** Update the issue status in the GRC platform:
  - **Open:** Issue identified; corrective action not yet started.
  - **In Progress:** Corrective action underway.
  - **Resolved:** Corrective action implemented and verified.
  - **Accepted:** Issue acknowledged but formally accepted as a risk (requires approval as per Risk Assessment Policy).
- **Reporting:** Follow-up findings and status are reported to the Security Officer and executive management, typically as part of the Security Oversight Committee's regular meeting cycle.

### Step 6: Assessment Close-Out

At the conclusion of each assessment cycle:

- **Final Report:** The Security Officer compiles a final assessment report including:
  - Executive summary.
  - Control effectiveness statistics.
  - Summary of issues identified and their status.
  - Trends compared to previous assessments.
  - Recommendations for process improvements.
- **Review:** The report is presented to executive management and/or the Security Oversight Committee.
- **Continuous Improvement:** Lessons learned from the assessment process are used to improve:
  - Control design and documentation.
  - Assessment methodology and efficiency.
  - Evidence collection and retention practices.

## Documentation and Evidence Retention

All documents related to control self-assessments must be retained for a minimum of ____ years (recommended: 3 years for SOC 2, longer if required by regulation):

- Assessment reports and findings.
- Evidence collected (logs, screenshots, reports, attestations).
- Action plans and verification records.
- Communications related to the assessment.

Documents must be stored in a secure, access-controlled location with backup (recommended: the GRC platform itself, or a dedicated compliance document repository).

## Process Review and Update

This process is reviewed annually by the Security Officer and ____ (e.g., CTO, CFO) to ensure its continued relevance and effectiveness. Any changes required are approved by executive management. The review considers:

- Changes in applicable compliance frameworks or regulations.
- Feedback from assessors and control owners.
- Findings from internal and external audits.
- Industry best practices for control assessment.

## Tools and Resources

The following tools support the control self-assessment process. Specific tool selections should be documented here:

- **GRC Platform (recommended: Drata, Vanta, or equivalent):** Central repository for controls, evidence collection, assessment tracking, and reporting. Provides framework mappings (SOC 2, ISO 27001, etc.) and automated evidence collection where possible.
- **Document Management System:** Repository for assessment reports, evidence files, and supporting documentation.
- **Ticketing / Work Tracking System:** Tracking of corrective actions and remediation tasks.
- **Communication Platform:** Coordination of assessment activities and notifications.
